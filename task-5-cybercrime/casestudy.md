# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI/Online Payment Fraud is a type of cybercrime where fraudsters trick users into transferring money to their accounts using fake payment requests. Typically, the crime occurs in the following steps: First, the fraudster contacts the victim via phone, SMS, or WhatsApp, claiming to be from a bank or payment platform. Next, they create a sense of urgency, such as a fake failed transaction, account verification, or reward claim. Then, the victim is instructed to share their UPI PIN or approve a payment request. Sometimes, phishing links are sent to steal login credentials.  

College students and young professionals are often targeted because they frequently use UPI apps for payments and may act quickly without verifying messages. Consequences include immediate loss of money from bank accounts, compromised personal data, and emotional stress. Victims may also face difficulty recovering funds due to delayed reporting or lack of awareness about official channels.  

Being aware of the methods used in such frauds, double-checking messages, and never sharing UPI PINs or OTPs can prevent falling victim. If fraud occurs, victims can report it to the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call Helpline 1930.
